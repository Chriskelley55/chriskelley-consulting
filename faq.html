Everything you want to know about AI automation for your business.

The Basics
What does an AI automation consultant do? +
An AI automation consultant maps how your business operates, identifies tasks that are being done manually but could run automatically, and builds the systems to close that gap. This includes things like lead follow-up, client onboarding, appointment reminders, reporting, and data entry — anything repetitive that takes time away from the actual work. At Chris Kelley Consulting, we start with a Workflow Audit to find the highest-value opportunities, then build from there.
What's the difference between AI automation and regular automation? +
Traditional automation follows fixed rules: if X happens, do Y. It's powerful but brittle — it breaks when something unexpected comes in. AI automation can handle variability. It can read an email and decide how to respond, score a lead based on what they wrote, extract data from a document in any format, or route a request based on context. We use both — traditional automation for predictable, high-volume tasks and AI for anything that requires judgment.
Do I need to be technical to use AI automation tools? +
No. The whole point of working with a consultant is that you don't have to be. You explain how your business works, what's taking the most time, and what good looks like — we handle the technical build. Most of our clients are contractors, trades businesses, and service companies with no internal tech team.
What is the best AI automation tool for small businesses? +
There's no single best tool — it depends on what your business already uses. Common tools we work with include n8n, Make.com, Zapier, and custom AI agents built with Claude or OpenAI's APIs. The tool matters less than the workflow design. A well-designed automation in any of these platforms will save more time than a poorly designed one in the "best" platform.
Can AI automation replace my employees? +
That's not what we're building. The goal is to give your people back the hours that repetitive work is stealing — so they can focus on customers, relationships, and the work that actually requires a human. Most of our clients don't reduce headcount after an automation buildout. They redirect time toward growth instead of admin.
