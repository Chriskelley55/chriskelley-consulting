The Starting Point

A good business
with no digital presence.

Kurt runs Imperial Water Co. — a water treatment, filtration, and softening service out of Sugar Land, TX. He's good at what he does. He was getting work through word of mouth. And he was tracking every single customer on notecards.

The problems

No website meant no way to get found online. No business email meant every quote went out from a personal Gmail. No contact form meant leads that came in after hours just... didn't. Competitors with even basic sites were winning jobs Kurt should have had.

The opportunity

Sugar Land is one of the fastest-growing suburbs in Texas. Hard water is a real problem — most Houston-area homes need treatment. Nobody dominant in the local search results. Kurt had the skills. He just needed the presence to match.

The Google Business Profile is the most important piece for a local service business

When someone searches "water softener Sugar Land" on Google Maps, the results that show up are GBP listings — not websites. Getting Kurt's profile verified and filled out is what starts feeding him into that local pack. It's free, and most competitors haven't done it right.

What's Next

The site is the foundation.
The system is what scales it.

Week one gets Imperial Water Co. visible and capturing leads. The next phases turn that presence into an operation that runs itself — customer reminders, automated follow-up, and on-site invoicing.

Phase 1 — Active

Site Launch + Lead Capture

6-page site. Lead capture tool. Contact forms. Business email. Google Search Console. GA4. Google Business Profile. Client dashboard. Everything live, indexed, and tracked.

Phase 2 — Next

CRM + SMS Reminders

Google Sheet to track clients — name, system type, last/next service date. Apps Script sends Kurt a text automatically when a customer is due for service. Set it and forget it.

Phase 3 — Later

iPad Invoicing

Review products with the customer on-site, ring up the bill on an iPad. Either Square (simplest) or a custom web app depending on workflow. No more invoicing from memory.
